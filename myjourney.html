My journey

I am fourteen now, but my school ID reads like a travel itinerary. Thanks to my moms ever-changing job, I have walked the halls of eight different schools across 5 cities—sometimes starting fresh in the middle of the term, backpack in hand and heart pounding.

.

Finding Home in the Unknown

Each time we moved, I said goodbye to classrooms where Id learned my classmates quirks, favorite lunch spots, and after-school hangouts. Walking into a brand-new school meant introducing myself to strangers, learning new routines, and figuring out where the best seat in class was all before I even knew the teachers name. It was scary at first, but over time I discovered that being the “new kid” taught me how to make friends quickly and spot kindness in small gestures: a shared pencil, an inviting smile, or a seat saved at lunch.

.

Lessons Beyond Textbooks

Switching schools so often didnt just sharpen my social skills—it showed me how to adapt. I learned to ask questions without embarrassment, jump into group projects with confidence, and navigate different teaching styles. I became resourceful, finding ways to keep up with varied curriculums and juggle new homework systems. More than anything, I learned resilience: even when the next move arrived on the horizon, I carried forward the friendships, lessons, and memories I'd made.

.

Looking Ahead

Moving eight times has shaped who I am: someone who sees change not as a setback, but as an opportunity to grow. Now, whenever I face a new challenge—whether its a surprise spelling quiz or joining a brand-new club—I remember the courage it took to step into a fresh classroom and promise myself: “You have done this before. You have got this again.”
